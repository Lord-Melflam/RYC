# Moderation Guidelines - RYC (Rate Your Courses)

**Version**: 1.0  
**Last Updated**: January 2026

## Purpose

RYC's moderation system ensures that course reviews are:
- Honest and helpful
- Respectful and constructive
- Free from spam and abuse
- Valuable for future students

## Layered Moderation Approach

RYC uses a multi-layered moderation system that balances automation, community involvement, and human oversight.

### Layer 1: Automated Filtering

All submitted reviews automatically go through:

1. **Content Validation**
   - Required fields check (rating, difficulty, workload)
   - Character limits enforcement
   - Format validation

2. **Spam Detection**
   - Duplicate content detection
   - Rate limiting enforcement
   - Suspicious pattern detection

3. **Initial Status**: All reviews start as `PENDING`

### Layer 2: Community Moderation

Users play a crucial role in maintaining quality:

1. **Flagging System**
   - Any user can flag a review
   - Must provide a reason
   - Flagging is anonymous

2. **Flag Thresholds**
   - 1 flag: Review marked for monitoring
   - 3+ flags: Review status changes to `FLAGGED`
   - Automatic escalation to manual review

3. **Flag Reasons**
   - Spam or promotional content
   - Offensive language or personal attacks
   - Misleading or false information
   - Duplicate content
   - Off-topic content
   - Other (with explanation)

### Layer 3: Manual Review

Human moderators review:
- Flagged content
- Randomly sampled reviews (quality assurance)
- Appeals (future feature)

## Review Status States

### PENDING
- **Definition**: Newly submitted review awaiting approval
- **Visibility**: Not visible to public

### APPROVED
- **Definition**: Review meets quality standards
- **Visibility**: Public, visible to all users
- **Can Change**: Yes, if flagged by community

### FLAGGED
- **Definition**: Community has raised concerns (3+ flags)
- **Visibility**: Hidden from public pending review
- **Next Steps**: Manual moderator review

### REJECTED
- **Definition**: Review violates guidelines
- **Visibility**: Permanently hidden
- **Next Steps**: None (not published)

## Content Guidelines

### âœ… Acceptable Content

**Helpful Reviews Should:**
- Be based on actual course experience
- Provide constructive feedback
- Include specific details when possible
- Balance positive and negative aspects
- Help future students make informed decisions

**Examples:**
- "Great course for beginners. Professor explains concepts clearly. Projects are challenging but fair."
- "Heavy workload, expect 10-15 hours/week. Difficult exams but well-prepared lectures."
- "Interesting material but disorganized. More structure would help."

### âŒ Unacceptable Content

**Reviews Will Be Rejected For:**

1. **Personal Attacks**
   - Insulting instructors or students
   - Name-calling or harassment
   - Discriminatory language
   - Threats or intimidation

2. **Spam**
   - Promotional content
   - Irrelevant information
   - Duplicate submissions
   - Nonsense or gibberish

3. **Offensive Content**
   - Profanity or vulgar language
   - Hate speech
   - Sexual content
   - Violent content

4. **False Information**
   - Deliberately misleading reviews
   - Reviews for courses not taken
   - Fabricated experiences
   - Competitive sabotage

5. **Privacy Violations**
   - Sharing personal information
   - Identifying specific students
   - Posting contact information

6. **Academic Integrity Issues**
   - Encouraging cheating
   - Sharing exam content
   - Promoting academic dishonesty

## Moderator Responsibilities

### What Moderators Do

1. **Review Flagged Content**
   - Examine flagged reviews within 24 hours
   - Make fair, consistent decisions
   - Document reasoning

2. **Quality Assurance**
   - Random sampling of approved reviews
   - Trend analysis
   - Pattern detection

3. **Policy Enforcement**
   - Apply guidelines consistently
   - Update procedures as needed
   - Report issues

4. **Privacy Protection**
   - Never attempt to identify users
   - Protect anonymous submissions
   - Maintain confidentiality

### What Moderators DON'T Do

- âŒ Censor negative (but fair) reviews
- âŒ Favor certain courses or departments
- âŒ Make subjective quality judgments
- âŒ Attempt to identify reviewers
- âŒ Share moderation decisions publicly

## Moderator Guidelines

### Decision Framework

When reviewing flagged content, ask:

1. **Is it relevant?**
   - Does it pertain to the course?
   - Is it helpful for future students?

2. **Is it respectful?**
   - Constructive criticism vs. personal attack?
   - Professional language?

3. **Is it honest?**
   - Appears genuine?
   - Based on real experience?

4. **Is it appropriate?**
   - Follows content guidelines?
   - No violations?
